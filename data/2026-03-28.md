### [1] [Characterization of Constraints in Flexible Unknown Environments](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24813)
*Samrat Bhattacharyya,Nabil Simaan*

Main category: cs.RO

TL;DR: 提出一种在线路径规划算法，用于在未知环境中安全自主操纵柔性约束物体，通过实时识别和表征感知到的柔性约束及全局刚度，实现同时探索、表征和操纵弹性系统。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 在未知环境中安全操纵柔性约束物体（如手术中的器官牵拉）需要实时识别约束特性，现有方法缺乏对柔性约束的实时感知和适应性规划能力。

Method: 基于局部力和位置信息的约束探索方法，在物体轨迹的多个位姿处考虑感知约束刚度，利用刚度特征向量信息，通过简单机械约束（如铰链、平面约束）的图集来表征和识别全局刚度行为。

Result: 通过仿真和实验验证了算法有效性，能够实时识别常见简单机械约束（如柔性铰链）并确定相关螺旋参数，展示了同时进行全局约束/刚度探索和安全操纵柔性约束物体的可行性。

Conclusion: 该方法为实现安全协作操纵（如手术中的器官牵拉和操作）提供了可行途径，通过同时探索、表征和操纵弹性系统，能够在未知环境中安全自主地处理柔性约束物体。

Abstract: This paper presents an online path planning algorithm for safe autonomous manipulation of a flexibly constrained object in an unknown environment. Methods for real time identification and characterization of perceived flexible constraints and global stiffness are presented. Used in tandem, these methods allow a robot to simultaneously explore, characterize, and manipulate an elastic system safely. Navigation without a-priori knowledge of the system is achieved using constraint exploration based on local force and position information. The perceived constraint stiffness is considered at multiple poses along an object's (system) trajectory. Using stiffness eigenvector information, global stiffness behavior is characterized and identified using an atlas of simple mechanical constraints, such as hinges and planar constraints. Validation of these algorithms is carried out by simulation and experimentally. The ability to recognize several common simple mechanical constraints (such as a flexible hinge) in real time, and to subsequently identify relevant screw parameters is demonstrated. These results suggest the feasibility of simultaneous global constrain/stiffness exploration and safe manipulation of flexibly constrained objects. We believe that this approach will eventually enable safe cooperative manipulation in applications such as organ retraction and manipulation during surgery

### [2] [A Mentalistic Interface for Probing Folk-Psychological Attribution to Non-Humanoid Robots](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25646)
*Giulio Pisaneschi,Pierpaolo Serio,Estelle Gerbier,Andrea Dan Ryals,Lorenzo Pollini,Mario G. C. A. Cimino*

Main category: cs.RO

TL;DR: 开发了一个实验平台，用于研究人们对非人形机器人意向状态归因，通过保持行为不变但改变解释框架（心理主义、目的论、机械论）来探究语言和框架如何影响意向立场的采用。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 研究语言和解释框架如何影响人们对机器人行为的意向状态归因，特别是在非人形机器人情境下，理解意向立场在机器人学中的形成机制。

Method: 构建了一个结合模拟机器人、真实任务环境和基于大语言模型的解释层的实验平台。平台能够在保持机器人行为不变的情况下，用心理主义（mentalistic）、目的论（teleological）或机械论（mechanistic）三种不同框架来解释相同行为。

Result: 该平台提供了一个受控的实验环境，能够系统地研究解释框架如何影响人们对机器人行为的意向状态归因，为理解意向立场在机器人交互中的形成提供了实验工具。

Conclusion: 通过开发这个实验平台，研究者能够探究语言和解释框架在塑造人们对机器人意向状态归因中的作用，为理解人类如何将意向立场应用于非人形机器人提供了新的研究方法。

Abstract: This paper presents an experimental platform for studying intentional-state attribution toward a non-humanoid robot. The system combines a simulated robot, realistic task environments, and large language model-based explanatory layers that can express the same behavior in mentalistic, teleological, or mechanistic terms. By holding behavior constant while varying the explanatory frame, the platform provides a controlled way to investigate how language and framing shape the adoption of the intentional stance in robotics.

### [3] [Drive My Way: Preference Alignment of Vision-Language-Action Model for Personalized Driving](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.25740)
*Zehao Wang,Huaide Jiang,Shuaiwu Dong,Yuping Wang,Hang Qiu,Jiachen Li*

Main category: cs.RO

TL;DR: Drive My Way (DMW)是一个个性化的视觉-语言-动作驾驶框架，通过用户嵌入学习和实时语言指令来适应个人驾驶习惯和意图。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 人类驾驶行为具有个性化特征，受长期习惯和短期意图影响。现有端到端自动驾驶系统要么优化通用目标，要么依赖固定驾驶模式，缺乏适应个人偏好或理解自然语言意图的能力。

Method: 提出DMW个性化视觉-语言-动作驾驶框架：1）从多个真实驾驶员的个性化驾驶数据集中学习用户嵌入；2）在规划时基于该嵌入条件化策略；3）自然语言指令提供额外的短期指导。

Result: 在Bench2Drive基准测试中的闭环评估显示，DMW改善了风格指令适应能力；用户研究表明其生成的行为可被识别为每个驾驶员自身的风格，突显个性化作为以人为中心的自动驾驶的关键能力。

Conclusion: 个性化是以人为中心的自动驾驶的关键能力，DMW框架通过结合长期驾驶习惯学习和实时语言指令适应，有效实现了驾驶行为的个性化定制。

Abstract: Human driving behavior is inherently personal, which is shaped by long-term habits and influenced by short-term intentions. Individuals differ in how they accelerate, brake, merge, yield, and overtake across diverse situations. However, existing end-to-end autonomous driving systems either optimize for generic objectives or rely on fixed driving modes, lacking the ability to adapt to individual preferences or interpret natural language intent. To address this gap, we propose Drive My Way (DMW), a personalized Vision-Language-Action (VLA) driving framework that aligns with users' long-term driving habits and adapts to real-time user instructions. DMW learns a user embedding from our personalized driving dataset collected across multiple real drivers and conditions the policy on this embedding during planning, while natural language instructions provide additional short-term guidance. Closed-loop evaluation on the Bench2Drive benchmark demonstrates that DMW improves style instruction adaptation, and user studies show that its generated behaviors are recognizable as each driver's own style, highlighting personalization as a key capability for human-centered autonomous driving. Our data and code are available at https://dmw-cvpr.github.io/.
